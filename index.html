Meeting The Brief

Investigation

Ideas
Some conceived ideas included a running tracker, hydration tracker and calorie tracker. I decided to select the sleep tracker. I will see how sleep length, air quality while sleeping and meditation affect heart rate, memory and perceived happiness. My device will track all these different inputs.

Existing solutions
The Fitbit Sense 2 is the best sleep tracker overall according to usnews.com1. The Fitbit Sense 2 is a wrist attached watch, detecting heart rate and motion and automatically tracking sleep quality. The Fitbit presents data such as sleep quality score and sleep length.

Wellbeing definition
“Wellbeing is present when a person realises their potential, is resilient in dealing with the normal stresses of their life, takes care of their physical wellbeing and has a sense of purpose, connection and belonging to a wider community. It is a fluid way of being and needs nurturing throughout life.”

How my project links to wellbeing
Heart rate, memory and perceived happiness are crucial to wellbeing.:

  • “an unusually high resting heart rate or low maximum heart rate may signify an increased risk of heart disease or other medical condition.”4. This emphasises the significance of heart rate in wellbeing.
  • “Without adequate sleep, your brain has a harder time absorbing and recalling new information.”5. Worse memory leads to people being less resilient in dealing with the normal stresses of life like schoolwork, work pressure, responsibilities.
  • “The risk of getting high blood pressure among happy people was 9% lower than among unhappy people.”8. This highlights not only the importance of happiness to emotional wellbeing, but also physical wellbeing.

Survey
I conducted a survey on sleep and the use of sleep trackers. I sent the survey out to students in my school - potential end users. The survey gathered insights into how sleep trackers may impact peoples sleep patterns and wellbeing and understanding of user needs. The results received were very promising:

  • 55% of participants get 7 hours or less sleep - the majority get less than the recommended amount of sleep for teenagers, which is 8 to 10 hours of sleep7.
  • 55% believe they do not get enough sleep.
  • 64% believe that their memory is affected by the amount of sleep they get.
  • 73% think that the amount of sleep they get does not affect their heart rate throughout the day.
  • 45% did not know a higher resting heart rate is associated with increased risk of heart disease.
  • Participants on average rated the air quality in their room as 6.27 out of 10.
  • 73% find bad air quality in their bedroom affects their sleep quality.
  • 73% do not use a device to track their sleep.
  • 55% believe a sleep tracker would help them improve their sleep.
  • 12% of people said they meditate.
  • 60% believe meditation helps with sleep quality.
These survey results show that a sleep tracker that helps users monitor their sleep duration and educates on the dangers of not getting good quality sleep is needed.

Plan and Design

Basic requirements

  1. I will use a microbit for my device - utilising both digital and analogue inputs and outputs. I will use air quality and an accelerometer as analogue inputs, and button ‘A’ and ‘B’ for digital inputs. I will also use the display of the microbit as a digital output and sound as an analogue output. The sleep tracker will automatically start tracking the length of sleep and air quality on user falling asleep. I will also create a heart rate tracker, memory test, perceived happiness entry program and meditation entry program.
  2. The data from the microbit will be sent through radio to another microbit connected to the PC via USB. This data will be processed and validated in Python and sent to CSV files.
  3. I will analyse the data using the mean, minimum and maximum of the data to inform the user of future decisions relating to wellbeing.

Advanced requirements

  1. I will create a dataset programmatically through Python, basing my model off this dataset. I will use linear regression using the scikit-learn library. My model will calculate heart rate, memory level and perceived happiness based off sleep length, air quality average while sleeping and if they have meditated that day. It will be capable of answering four ‘what if’ type questions. The questions will be:
    • What if my sleep length, air quality while sleeping and have I meditated that day is at baseline, what will my heart rate, memory level and perceived happiness be?
    • (Assuming I don’t meditate most days) What if my sleep length and air quality while sleeping is at baseline and I have meditated that day, what will my heart rate, memory level and perceived happiness be?
    • What if my sleep length and have I meditated that day is at baseline and air quality while sleeping is 50% better than baseline, what will my heart rate, memory level and perceived happiness be?
    • What if air quality while sleeping and have I meditated that day is at baseline and sleep length is 50% better than baseline, what will my heart rate, memory level and perceived happiness be?
  2. My what if questions have three parameters. There are 2 floats, sleep length and air quality and there is 1 boolean ,have I meditated that day.
  3. My device will display bar charts comparing the results of the ‘what if’ questions. My device will also display in a line graph the users progress in sleep length, air quality while sleeping, meditation, heart rate, memory and perceived happiness.

Approach taken
My project will utilise the Agile approach as opposed to Waterfall approach. Agile allows for more flexibility, adaptability and iteration enabling identification of issues early in development process.

I decided to use modular design over top-down design, as modular design simplifies the project down into easier to manage modules, modular design allows me to use the modules of code in other parts of the project and the modules are small, so there will be less bugs and they will be easier to fix.
